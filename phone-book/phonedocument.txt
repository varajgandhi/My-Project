Antonio Meucci(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antonio_Meucci), 1854, constructed telephone-like devices.

Johann Philipp Reis(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Philipp_Reis), 1860, constructed prototype 'make-and-break' telephones, today called Reis telephone(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reis_telephone).


Alexander Graham Bell(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Graham_Bell) was awarded the first U.S. patent for the invention of the telephone in 1876.

Elisha Gray(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elisha_Gray), 1876, designed a telephone using a water microphone(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_microphone) in Highland Park, Illinois.

Tivadar Puskás(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tivadar_Pusk%C3%A1s) proposed the telephone switchboard exchange(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telephone_exchange) in 1876.


Thomas Edison(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Edison) invented the carbon microphone(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_microphone) which produced a strong telephone signal.
